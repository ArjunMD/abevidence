# MyEvidence
For clinicians who love Pubmed, but hate having 37 tabs open and trying to track what they've read... who love guidelines, but can't read through 80-page PDFs to find the parts relevant to them.

## What you can do
- Build a repository of pertinent abstracts and guidelines.
- Save PubMed abstracts by PMID. The app auto-extracts key details.
- Upload guideline PDFs. The app extracts recommendations and organizes them into categories.
- Search and browse your database.
- Search PubMed and track your progress with a ledger.

## Pages
- **PMID → Abstract**: Enter a PMID, hit fetch. The app pulls the abstract and extracts PICO details, patient count, author's conclusion, and specialty. Edit anything before saving. Related papers show up via PubMed and Semantic Scholar, and there's a clipboard to queue up PMIDs you want to check next.
- **Upload Guideline**: Upload a guideline PDF. Title, year, and specialty are auto-extracted. Recommendations get pulled out and sorted into categories, with likely duplicates pushed to the end. You can tweak the display, but be careful not to break formatting.
- **Single-study view**: Look at one record in full detail. Search for it by text, edit metadata, see related studies, and link it to guidelines.
- **Browse studies**: Scroll through everything you've saved. Filter by specialty, year, or search text. Toggle between specialty-grouped and year-grouped views.
- **Search PubMed**: Pick a year, month, specialty, and journal — see all results. Journals and study types are limited to high-impact and high-evidence ones on purpose. Mark papers as irrelevant or send them to the PMID → Abstract page. The ledger tracks what you've already searched.
- **Manage**: Delete abstracts or guidelines. Also where you go to edit metadata after the fact.
- **About**: This page.
- **History**: Timeline of everything you've added, sorted newest first.

## Evidence Workflows
1. **Research a topic from one strong seed paper**
   - Find a relevant article in PubMed and copy its PMID.
   - Open **PMID → Abstract**, fetch the paper, review the extracted fields, and save it to your database.
   - Use **PubMed Related articles** and **Semantic Scholar similar papers** to branch outward.
   - Use the **Clipboard** expander to keep track of PMIDs you want to open next.
   - Repeat with a new seed paper

2. **Get up to date on recent research**
   - Open **Search PubMed**.
   - Review the ledger to see which month/specialty/journal combinations you already searched.
   - Search uncovered gaps, then send promising papers to **PMID → Abstract** and save what matters. Clear what doesn't.
